2020 Presidential Election

This ArcGIS StoryMap explores the intricate relationship between median income levels and voting patterns in the 2020 U.S. presidential election. Using maps created in ArcGIS Pro and polished with Adobe Illustrator, the webpage visualizes how economic disparities across states influenced political outcomes. States with higher income levels demonstrated a preference for the Democratic candidate, while lower-income states leaned Republican. Beyond presenting statistical correlations, the story delves into broader socio-economic and cultural dynamics that shape political landscapes. By analyzing trends and patterns, this project offers a nuanced perspective on the intersection of income inequality and electoral behavior, making complex data accessible and engaging through a blend of maps, visuals, and interactive storytelling tools. Explore the factors that define the political geography of America in this comprehensive visual narrative.
